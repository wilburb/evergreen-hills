The Fraser Fir has short (approximately 1”) shiny dark green needles with silvery undersides. When properly watered, needle retention is excellent. Branches are sturdy, upturned slightly, and spaced in a way that makes the tree easier to decorate than many others and perfect for showcasing ornaments. The tree’s profile is generally slender.

The Balsam Fir is very similar to the Fraser Fir, with slightly paler green needles and more flexible branches. It is more aromatic as well, and maintains its very pleasant fragrance throughout the Christmas season.
